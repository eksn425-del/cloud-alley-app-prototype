# Drone Route Constraints

## Purpose

This document defines the constraints that should guide a future low-altitude route feasibility module.

## Spatial Constraints

- Minimum horizontal clearance from building facade.
- Minimum clearance from balcony projection.
- Safe distance from resident windows and private outdoor areas.
- Avoidance of dense pedestrian nodes.
- Reasonable landing point distance from residential cores.
- Separation between drone corridor and shared leisure areas.

## Environmental Constraints

- Wind speed and wind direction.
- Facade wind pressure.
- Rain or low-visibility risk.
- Tree canopy or landscape obstruction.
- Noise-sensitive zones.

## Service Constraints

- Delivery time.
- Number of households covered.
- Delivery node capacity.
- Package pickup convenience.
- Resident preference for contactless or staffed pickup.

## Design Constraints

- Balcony depth should support daily use without creating route conflict.
- Building spacing should not only serve UAV clearance; it must also support daylight, privacy, and pedestrian scale.
- Masterplan logistics nodes should remain legible to residents.
- Low-altitude service should be integrated into community life rather than feeling like external infrastructure.

## Product Interpretation

The best framing is not "AI designed the drone route." A stronger and more honest framing is:

```text
AI helped reason through route feasibility, building spacing, balcony projection, and resident-service trade-offs, then the app prototype expressed the resulting low-altitude community scenario.
```
