# Interview Talking Points

## 30-second Version

Cloud Alley is a low-altitude smart community app prototype connected to a residential design project. I would not overclaim it as a strong AI product. Its AI relevance is in the design decision process: I used AI to reason through trade-offs among drone routes, building spacing, balcony projection depth, and masterplan layout, then translated those decisions into a resident-facing H5 prototype.

## 60-second Version

This project started as a residential design, but I extended it into a product prototype for a low-altitude smart community. The app shows delivery tracking, balcony environment status, shared spaces, community notices, and low-carbon behavior. The AI-related part is not a mature algorithm yet; it is a design-support workflow. I used AI to compare how drone routes, balcony projection, building spacing, and planning layout influence each other. For example, a deeper balcony may improve resident use but increase route conflict and privacy risk; a closer landing point improves service efficiency but may create pedestrian conflict. I used that reasoning to shape the app scenario and product narrative.

## How to Answer "Is This an AI Product?"

I would say it is AI-adjacent rather than a complete AI product. It is currently a product prototype with AI-assisted spatial decision reasoning behind it. The next upgrade would be a low-altitude route feasibility module that scores drone route risk based on clearance, balcony projection, wind, privacy, and pedestrian conflict.

## Avoid Overclaiming

- Do not say AI automatically generated the residential design.
- Do not say this is a validated drone control system.
- Do not say it is stronger than the light-right repair or Spatial AI projects.
- Do not claim real deployment.
